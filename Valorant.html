The lastest news from the world of eSports!

Tuesday 24th November 2020

Riot announces Valorant Champions Tour 2021

Today Riot Games, creators of Valorant, announced the first international tournament for Valorant. This tournament will run a season long competition in 2021 to determine the best Valorant team in the world, and will involve Europe, North America, South America, Asia, Africa and Australia. “The Champions Tour is our next step to grow Valorant into a global esport worthy of the passion of our fans,” said Whalen Rozelle, senior director of esports at Riot Games.

Friday 11th December 2020

Cloud 9 Blue Valorant Team Update

Today, North American esport organisation Cloud 9 released a statement updating thier fans on their Valorant starting roster. Tyson 'TenZ' Ngo, Skyler 'Relyks' Weaver and Mitch 'mitch' Semago are now joined by Daniel 'Vice' Kim for 2021. This marks the departure of Josh 'Shinobi' Abastado from the team.

Sunday 6th December 2020

100 Thieves Crowned Valorant NA First Strike Champions

Today, North American esport organisation 100 Thieves beat TSM 3-1 to win the First Strike tournament. 100T also beat T1 and Sentinels in the quater and semi finals to reach the grand finals where their impressive performance earned them a commanding victory. With 300,000 peak viewers online, 100T claimed their $40,000 prize while TSM won $20,000.

Sunday 6th December 2020

Team Heretics Crowned Valorant EU First Strike Champions

Today, European esport organisation Team Heretics beat SUMN FC 3-1 to win the First Strike tournament. Heretics also beat Team Liquid and G2 in the quater and semi finals to reach the grand finals where their impressive performance earned them a commanding victory. With 106,000 peak viewers online, Heretics claimed their $40,000 prize while SUMN FC won $20,000.
